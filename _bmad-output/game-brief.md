# Game Brief: Gnoy Simulator

**Date:** 2026-05-03
**Author:** Cpain
**Status:** Draft for GDD Development

---

## Executive Summary

**Gnoy Simulator** is a satirical top-down 2D RPG set in an alt-Earth present-day dystopia ruled by a malignant oligarch class — the Xyoners — whose grip is maintained through fast food, social media addiction, wage slavery, religious extremism, debt, and surveillance. The player begins as a fully-compliant "Gnoy" Wageworker — eating slop, doom-scrolling the feed, clocking into a job at a company that does nothing — until a staged news event cracks their reality open. From there, the game becomes a freeform investigation-simulator-action hybrid: document the Xyoner network, recruit the awakening, survive Heat, and decide what to do with the truth you uncover.

The signature design innovation is the **Truth Paradox**: the more correct your evidence, the more dangerous and discredited you become simultaneously. Publishing strong evidence raises Heat (operative attention); publishing weak evidence costs Credibility (public trust). Three interacting meters — NPC Trust, Credibility, Heat — make *framing* the core skill expression. This is fused with a **Dossier System / Politburo Simulation / Reputation Web** triad that creates a living adaptive enemy faction without infringing the Warner Bros Nemesis patent (US 10,926,179).

The game runs on a Persona/Stardew day-cycle skeleton with finite action slots, Hotline Miami real-time top-down combat, and Disco Elysium-depth dialogue. The same 7-attribute, 24-skill RPG foundation underlies all three gameplay layers, so a build that excels at investigation plays the action and simulator layers radically differently than one built for combat or social manipulation. A signature **Music Deterioration Mechanic** has corporate jingles in Xyoner spaces audibly corrupt as the player's Awakening Level rises — by Level 7, McXyon's brand music sounds like a damaged file. The player literally hears the manipulation become visible.

Tone is satirical/comedic in the Fallout tradition — dark humor, absurdist corporate evil, equal-opportunity satire of documented extremist ideologies (Christian, Muslim, Jewish — ideology-targeted, not religion-targeted). Five factions, 13 boss archetypes, an Inner Ring endgame reveal, 12 districts, 5 endings, and the reality that ignoring the Xyoners entirely and just living a Gnoy life is itself a valid playthrough. **The game judges nothing. The systems generate your story.**

## Game Vision

### Core Concept

A satirical RPG simulator where the player wakes up inside a dystopia they didn't notice they were living in, and decides — moment by moment, day by day — how much of the truth they can stand to see, document, broadcast, and act on. The game's central design premise is that the systems themselves enact the satire: subscription cancellations *are* the awakening, slop healing *is* the system rewarding compliance with long-term decay, NPC Mode degrading at high Fatigue *is* the cost of clarity. Mechanics don't illustrate the theme — they *are* the theme.

Underneath sits a full RPG skeleton. The investigation layer (Disco Elysium × Obra Dinn), the simulator layer (Persona × Stardew), and the action layer (Hotline Miami × Deus Ex) all share the same 7 attributes, 24 skills, 40 talents, and disposition axes. A choice in any layer affects the other two.

### Elevator Pitch

> **"Disco Elysium meets Hotline Miami in a Fallout-flavored alt-Earth where the conspiracy is real, the truth is punished, and the conspiracy theorists in your forum bookmarks were right about everything."**

You start as a goyslop-pilled wageworker. You witness one staged news event. You spend the rest of the game deciding what to do about it — and the game lets you do nothing, if that's what you choose.

### Vision Statement

To ship a game that makes the player viscerally feel the *texture* of modern manufactured consent — the snooze button, the auto-deducted subscription, the Feed segment about "anti-Xyonetic extremists," the cheerful billboard you walk past every day — and then offers them a complete RPG with which to either fight it, document it, exploit it, or surrender to it. The game should be funny, ugly, replayable, talked-about, and structurally incapable of being mistaken for a power fantasy.

**Pillars of vision:**

1. **The satire IS the design.** Every mechanic must reinforce the thematic point or be cut.
2. **Simulator first, story second.** Every playthrough is unique. The awakening path is available, never mandatory.
3. **Build-gated world access.** More Awake = more map, more dialogue, more world. The Full Gnoy and the Awakened player are playing different games on the same map.
4. **Truth has a cost.** The Truth Paradox is the spine. Mechanics that contradict it get cut.
5. **The world doesn't revolve around the player.** Politburo Simulation runs whether the player acts or not.

## Target Market

### Primary Audience

**The Disco Elysium / Obra Dinn / Papers Please CRPG-investigation crowd, ages ~22–40.** Players who want:

- Deep dialogue with real consequence and visible skill checks.
- Reading-heavy, lore-dense gameplay with adult themes.
- Politically literate satire — they want the satire to *bite*, not be soft.
- Investigation as a core mechanic, not a quest type.
- Permadeath / Ironman-friendly systems for replayability.
- Signature mechanics they can describe to friends ("the music corrupts as you wake up").

This player has likely played: **Disco Elysium, Outer Wilds, Return of the Obra Dinn, Papers Please, Fallout: New Vegas, Pathologic 2, Pentiment, Citizen Sleeper, The Stanley Parable, Kentucky Route Zero.**

### Secondary Audience

**The Hotline Miami / Katana Zero / streetwise action crowd** drawn in by the combat layer; the **Stardew Valley / Persona / Spiritfarer** simulator-rhythm crowd drawn in by the day-cycle and homebase progression; and the **Edgy Indie / Cruelty Squad / LISA / Disco-adjacent meme** crowd drawn in by tone, satire, and the cultural-conversation hook of the subject matter.

The game has natural overlap with:
- **The "I just want to be left alone" simulator audience** (Stardew, Story of Seasons) — they may engage primarily with the day cycle and Slopside neighborhood life.
- **The CRPG build-craft audience** (Pathfinder, Pillars of Eternity) — they will engage with the 24-skill / 40-talent system as a sandbox.
- **Streamers and longform-content YouTubers** — the Schrödinger's NPC system, multiple endings, and the music deterioration mechanic are designed to generate clip-worthy moments.

### Market Context

The investigation-RPG niche (Disco Elysium, Obra Dinn, Pentiment, Citizen Sleeper) is established and profitable but underserved — most successful entries are small-team, modest-budget, and command devoted audiences willing to pay full price and replay. The political-satire RPG slot has been largely vacant since Fallout: New Vegas (2010). The combination of (a) modern media-criticism subject matter, (b) full RPG simulator, (c) signature unique mechanics (Truth Paradox, Music Deterioration, Schrödinger's NPCs), and (d) Hotline-Miami-grade action-loop credibility positions this title in a genre intersection that no current shipping game occupies.

**Cultural moment:** Skepticism of legacy media, awareness of algorithmic manipulation, declining trust in institutions, and the meme-fluency of online culture create a pre-built audience for the game's themes. The risk inversion: the game must avoid being read as partisan tract — equal-opportunity satire of all factional ideologies is structurally enforced by the design (Christian, Muslim, Jewish religious-extremist boss archetypes, plus the Vault and Cage which are ecumenical).

## Game Fundamentals

### Core Gameplay Pillars

1. **Simulator First, Story Second.** Every playthrough is unique. The awakening path is available, not mandatory. The systems generate your story; you don't follow a scripted one. Living a full Gnoy life is a valid run.
2. **Full RPG Skeleton.** Character stats, item stats, abilities, deep branching dialogue with real consequence. Investigation/documentation sits ON TOP of a full RPG foundation. Truth-telling, slop resistance, and social influence are character builds, not just narrative choices.
3. **Nemesis-Style Living World (Legally Distinct).** Xyoner agents, factions, and NPCs track your actions and respond dynamically — but memory lives in a faction dossier and an independent political simulation, not in individual NPCs. Operatives you destroy stay destroyed. Their *role* gets succession-filled.
4. **The Truth Paradox.** The more correct your evidence, the more dangerous and discredited you become simultaneously. Framing is the skill expression. This pillar invalidates any mechanic that rewards truth-telling in the conventional way.
5. **Build-Gated World Access.** More Awake = more map (sneak routes), more dialogue (internal voices), more world (Xyoner symbols become readable). The character growing literally changes what game you are playing.

### Primary Mechanics

**RPG Core**

- **7 Attributes:** BODY, MIND, SOUL, MOUTH, GHOST, GUT, SIGNAL
- **6 Derived Stats:** Credibility (SOUL+MOUTH), Heat (inverse GHOST), Awareness (MIND+SOUL), Slop Damage (ongoing), Reach (SIGNAL+MOUTH), Fatigue (passive accumulation), Cope (GUT+SOUL — mental resilience)
- **24 Skills** organized under the 6 active attributes (full list in brainstorming session, locked)
- **40 Talents** across 7 archetypes: Combat (9), Stealth/Infiltration (6), Information Warfare (6), Network/Social (6), Awakening (5), Economy/Resources (5), Wildcard (3)
- **2 Disposition Axes:** GNOY ↔ AWAKE, PASSIVE ↔ REBEL
- **Awakening Track:** Secondary progression, levels 1–10, mythic-equivalent. Cinematic moments at 1/5/10. In-world events + internal monologue at the rest.

**Core Loop (Three Layers)**

- **Micro (minutes):** Follow a thread → find a piece → decide what to do with it.
- **Meso (one session):** Day cycle + Heat drama + thread resolution.
- **Macro (campaign):** Awakening arc + faction destruction + Inner Ring endgame.

**Day Cycle**

- Morning / Afternoon / Evening + optional risky Night slot.
- Snooze button costs the morning slot silently. Quitting the job opens the day but spikes financial pressure.
- Sleep advances the world clock. Politburo Simulation ticks.

**Survival Pressure (Light, Thematic)**

- Monthly bills: rent + slop subscriptions (streaming, delivery, social premium, unused gym, supplements).
- **Each subscription cancelled = an Awakening Track moment AND severs a Feed connection AND adds a "possible radicalization" flag to the dossier.**
- Full Gnoy player is bled dry monthly. Endgame Awakened player has near-zero recurring expenses — they have shed the slop economy entirely.

**Heat System**

- Push as far as possible until Heat forces you dark.
- Per-district Heat tracking — district rotation is mature gameplay.
- Cooldown periods = life maintenance, cover work, relationship tending. Built-in breathing rhythm.

**Combat (Hotline Miami DNA)**

- Real-time top-down, ultra-fast, extreme lethality, instant restart within an encounter (world state persists).
- Stats modify parameters (BODY = hit threshold, Gymmaxx = speed, Hands = damage). Never invincibility.
- **Capture mechanic:** Downed → YIELD (guaranteed capture, GUT check limits dossier damage) or ATTEMPT ESCAPE (GHOST + Gymmaxx check, gear can bypass).
- **Faction counter-system:** Dossier reads your build patterns. Always ghost? Cage deploys thermal sensors. Always broadcast? Feed builds counter-narrative faster.
- **Signature weapons:** Evidence Brick, Slop Bag, Megaphone, Signal Jammer, Camera Flash, Fake ID Packet.
- **Goyslop healing:** Slop restores BODY immediately, no in-moment penalty. Slop Damage is a slow-accumulation track — long-term decision, not instant punishment.

**Equipment**

- 15 WoW-style slots. Outfit set bonuses (full civilian = Normie Cosplay bonus; full tactical = combat bonus + zero social viability).
- Tier ladder: Gnoy-grade → Standard → Underground → Resistance-crafted → Xyoner-seized (highest stats but raises Heat to carry).

**Investigation UI (Three Layers)**

- **Physical Board** (homebase wall) — pins, strings, photos, drag-and-connect. Macro picture.
- **Dossier Interface** (per evidence) — desk-style cross-reference, stamp evidence (Verified / Uncertain / Planted), publication interface (platform × framing × audience), preview Credibility-vs-Heat before commit.
- **Thought Cabinet** — mental threads, limited active simultaneously. Awakening Track reveals new interpretations of *existing* evidence as levels rise.
- **Connection Mechanic:** Player draws string between two pieces → skill check fires ([X] Fatigue / Rabbit Hole / Lore Depth / Glowie Sense). Three outcomes: critical success, standard success, fail. Wrong connections stay ambiguous; the Cage plants false ones; publishing wrong = Feed weaponizes.
- **War Room** (HQ Stage 3+, Analyst-managed): network graph, map overlay, live news ticker, Heat map, Dossier panel.

**Dialogue (Disco × Owlcat × Baldur's Gate 3 DNA)**

- Skill-check DC visible, consequence hidden (kills save-scum, preserves build expression).
- Internal voices (skills speaking) scale with Awakening + Fatigue.
- 3–5+ paths per dialogue goal, gated by different skill builds.
- Conversation log weaponized: NPCs quote you back, Cage interrogates spoken-with Gnoym whose memory enters your dossier verbatim, Feed edits soundbites.
- Item-drop in dialogue unlocks lines that don't exist without physical evidence.
- **Dialogue-as-combat:** The Trusted Anchor and Debt Dealer are pure dialogue bosses — multi-stage composure HP, evidence + skill combos, failure = blown cover + lost evidence + Heat spike.

**The Three Tracking Systems (legally distinct from WB Nemesis patent US 10,926,179)**

- **Player Dossier:** Single shared file maintained by Xyoner network intelligence. ALL operatives access the same dossier — no personal NPC memory in the faction system.
- **Politburo Simulation:** Independent political simulation — rivalries, succession, blackmail debts, factional alliances. Runs whether the player exists or not. Player actions are *inputs*; simulation rules determine outcomes.
- **Reputation Web:** Gnoym NPCs personally remember you (legally safe — no faction hierarchy). They gossip, reputation spreads, their interrogated memories enter your dossier verbatim.

**Quest Structure (7 parallel types, no markers default)**

1. Main Quest — "The Arrangement" (player-driven, no timer, no markers).
2. Faction Boss Investigations (13).
3. Recruit Personal Quests.
4. Emergent Politburo Events.
5. Reputation Web Side Quests.
6. Awakening Track Story Beats.
7. District Liberation Goals.

The Quest Journal is the character's own notes — the game does not auto-log objectives. Optional Markers Mode for accessibility.

**5 Endings:** The Public Reckoning / The Shadow Replacement / The Burn / The Long Game / The Sleep.

### Player Experience Goals

The player should feel:

- **Complicit, then queasy, then awake.** The first 15 minutes (the contextual tutorial, "A Perfectly Normal Morning") teaches every system silently — the snooze button steals the morning slot, the slop healing ticks an unlabeled grey number, the auto-deducted subscriptions appear without commentary, the Feed's segment about the staged event is the first time the central premise is *demonstrated* rather than narrated.
- **Like the world has its own life.** The Politburo runs in the background. NPCs have routines. Press cycles play whether the player tunes in or not. Missing things is possible — cold cases are harder but not impossible.
- **Alone with a real choice.** Five endings exist. The Sleep ending — give up, eat slop, watch feed — is supported. The game does not tell the player what they should do.
- **Replayable through identity, not content.** A Ghost build, a Grifter build, a Hands build, and a Touch-Grass build play radically different games on the same map.
- **That something cracked.** The Music Deterioration Mechanic — corporate jingles in Xyoner spaces audibly corrupting as Awakening rises — is designed as the signature emotional moment players will describe to friends.

## Scope and Constraints

### Target Platforms

- **Primary:** PC (Steam) — Windows / Linux. The reading-heavy, mouse-and-keyboard-friendly, modding-receptive natural home for this kind of CRPG-sim hybrid.
- **Likely secondary (post-launch):** Steam Deck (verified), macOS, Nintendo Switch (top-down 2D scales well).
- **Possible tertiary:** PS5 / Xbox if controller-first UX work is funded.
- **Engine:** Decision deferred to architecture phase. Realistic candidates: Unity (broad 2D RPG tooling, Steam Deck verified pipeline), Godot (open-source, strong 2D, growing CRPG ecosystem), Unreal (only if 3D-adjacent ambitions arise — currently not warranted).

### Development Timeline

**Status: To Be Determined** — solo / small-indie scope assumption pending team decision. Realistic ranges given the design footprint:

- **Vertical slice (full systems × one district × ~3 hours of play):** 6–12 months solo, 3–6 months with a small team.
- **Early-access launch (3–4 districts, full RPG systems, 1 ending playable):** 18–30 months from vertical slice.
- **Full launch (12 districts, 13 bosses, 5 endings, full content):** 30–48 months from vertical slice.

These are designer-realistic estimates for the scope as locked. Aggressive content cuts (fewer districts, fewer bosses, single ending at launch) can pull these in significantly. **This is a known risk and is addressed in Risk Assessment.**

### Budget Considerations

- Solo / micro-indie cost structure assumed unless team scales up. Primary cost line items: living expenses for the developer(s), audio composition (the music deterioration mechanic alone implies multi-version recordings of every Xyoner-space track), narrative design/writing (high-volume dialogue with skill-check branching), and pixel/sprite art (12 districts of detailed environmental satire).
- **Audio is the under-budgeted line item.** The Music Deterioration Mechanic, layered context-music system, and sound-as-gameplay (footsteps, broadcast quality, homebase ambient growth) require either a dedicated composer or a substantial outsourced budget.
- Marketing budget assumed to lean on organic / content-driven reach (signature mechanics + cultural moment + streamer-friendly design). Paid marketing minimal pre-launch.
- Funding paths to evaluate: Kickstarter (audience archetype is Kickstarter-receptive), publisher signing (Devolver/Annapurna/Raw Fury/11 bit are tonal fits), grants, or self-funded EA.

### Team Resources

**Status: To Be Determined.** Minimum viable team for the locked design:

- 1× Designer/Director (likely Cpain)
- 1× Programmer (or dual-role with designer if technical)
- 1× Pixel / 2D Artist (sprites, environments, UI)
- 1× Writer (dialogue volume is enormous — Disco-Elysium-tier word count)
- 1× Composer / Sound Designer (the deterioration mechanic alone justifies a dedicated role)
- Optional: 1× Systems Designer to own the simulation/dossier/politburo balancing.

Solo development is *technically* possible but would likely require 6+ years and aggressive content cuts. A 3–5 person core team is the realistic minimum for the design as locked.

### Technical Constraints

- **2D top-down, district-based (not open world).** Districts are detailed maps connected via thematic transitions. This is a deliberate scope-control decision — open world would require an order-of-magnitude larger art and design budget.
- **Pixel art or hand-drawn 2D sprites** — not 3D. Reduces art pipeline complexity.
- **Politburo Simulation must run cheaply in the background.** The design assumes it ticks weekly (in-game), which means it can be a relatively coarse-grained simulation (not real-time) — a tractable engineering problem.
- **Save system:** autosave at day boundaries + key events. Optional Ironman mode (one file, permanent death). Day-boundary saves bound the worst-case loss.
- **Content scaling:** the Reputation Web (every Gnoym remembers what you said) and the conversation log (your own words quoted back) are the highest-risk performance areas — needs early prototyping.
- **No live-service requirements.** Single-player, offline-capable. Significantly simplifies infra.

### Competitive Analysis

**Direct competitors:** None currently shipping. The intersection of (full RPG simulator + investigation core + politically literate satire + 2D top-down + signature unique mechanics) is genre-vacant.

**Adjacent competitors and how this title differentiates:**

- **Disco Elysium 2 (in development at ZA/UM derivatives):** Will share dialogue-CRPG audience. Differentiation: action layer, day cycle, faction simulation, modern alt-Earth setting.
- **The Outer Worlds (Obsidian) / Avowed:** AAA-budget RPGs with satirical leanings. Differentiation: 2D top-down, indie-budget, sharper satire, simulator depth.
- **Future "Shadow of Mordor"-style nemesis games:** Will share the adaptive-faction audience. Differentiation: legally distinct dossier system, satirical setting, full RPG layer.
- **Suzerain / The Forgotten City / Pentiment:** Narrative-CRPGs with strong systems. Differentiation: action layer, larger world, faction-simulation depth.

### Key Differentiators

1. **The Truth Paradox.** No shipping game punishes correct evidence with discrediting. This is the single most defensible design innovation.
2. **The Dossier / Politburo / Reputation Web triad.** A legally distinct nemesis-style adaptive faction with thematic superiority for the setting (a surveillance bureaucracy, not a personal nemesis).
3. **The Music Deterioration Mechanic.** Corporate music in Xyoner spaces audibly corrupts as the player awakens. Player *hears* the manipulation. Likely to be the thing players describe to friends.
4. **Subscription cancellations as mechanical awakening.** The slop economy is itself a system — shedding it IS the awakening, mechanically represented. No other RPG mechanizes this.
5. **The Schrödinger's NPC system (Dave).** First dialogue choice with one NPC permanently determines that NPC's role across the campaign — recruit, immovable Gnoy, or Glowie — without the player knowing. Replayability and social-content hook in one.
6. **The Sleep ending.** A major RPG that lets you give up, eat slop, and credits-roll years later asleep. Most RPGs cannot ship this ending. This one structurally requires it.
7. **Build-gated world geography.** Sneak routes, internal voices, readable Xyoner symbols, deeper map overlays unlock with character growth — the world literally expands as you wake up.
8. **The satire IS the mechanics.** Not narrative satire over generic gameplay. Mechanical satire — the systems themselves are the critique.

## Content Framework

### World and Setting

**Alt-Earth, present-day.** Recognizable but slightly wrong. Not sci-fi, not historical, not post-apocalyptic — just the world as it currently is, with the names changed, the satire dialed up, and the conspiracy real.

**The Xyoners** — the malignant ruling oligarch class. Land theft, child killing, political blackmail, media capture, political purchase. Five operational arms (factions). An Inner Ring whose number, names, and identities are unknown until endgame. The Xyoners are a fictional class, not tied to any real ethnic or religious group.

**The Gnoym** — the population. Most are fully compliant — eating slop, watching the feed, clocking in. Some are awakening. Some are already awake and underground. The player begins as a Gnoy.

**The Five Factions:**

- **The Trough** — food, agribusiness, processed consumption (Innovation Czar, Soil Baron, Revolving Door)
- **The Feed** — media, algorithms, narrative control (Engagement Architect, Trusted Anchor, Fact Checker)
- **The Pulpit** — religion, ideology, NGOs, wellness (Prosperity Prophet, Think Tank Sage, Settlement Ideologue, Jihadist Franchise Operator)
- **The Cage** — enforcement, surveillance, kompromat (Intelligence Artisan, Glowie)
- **The Vault** — finance, debt, capital (Benevolent Billionaire, Debt Dealer, Number Go Up Guy)

Faction Standing ladder: UNKNOWN → FLAGGED → OBSERVED → ASSET → COMPROMISED → OWNED. Double-cross tests at ASSET+. Cross-faction interference. The Vault never fully hostile. The Cage has pre-existing rivalry with The Feed (useful for manipulation).

**12 Districts** organized in 3 tiers:

- **Tier 1 — Major (multi-sub-area):** Slopside, The Cubicle Belt, The Plaza, The Vault District, The Garden
- **Tier 2 — Specialty:** The Pulpit Quarter, University Row, The Slop Belt, The Civic Center, The Hollow, The Outskirts
- **Tier 3 — Endgame:** The Compound (walled Inner Ring enclave)

Plus hidden Cage Black Sites and Inner Ring private locations (mansions, yachts, retreats — endgame only).

**Homebase Evolution (4 stages):**

1. **The Apartment** (Awake 1–2) — solo, evidence board, underground forum, slop fridge, vulnerable landlord
2. **The Office** (Awake 3–4) — 2–3 recruits, cover business lease, low-range pirate rig, paper-trail vulnerability
3. **The Underground HQ** (Awake 5–7) — full operation, war room with live dossier board, broadcast tower, single-location vulnerability
4. **The Network** (Awake 8+) — distributed safe house cells, no single point of failure, coordination complexity = new vulnerability

### Narrative Approach

**Player-authored, system-generated.** The Main Quest ("The Arrangement") is loose — an Inner Ring investigation with no timer, no markers, no urgency. Endgame triggers when *the player decides* they have enough.

**Tonal benchmarks:** Fallout (dark humor, absurdist evil), They Live (normal until you see it), American Beauty (perfection over rot), Don't Look Up (deadpan satirical present), Disco Elysium (skills as voices, satire by detail), Cruelty Squad (the world is grotesque on purpose).

**Equal-opportunity satire** of documented extremist ideologies (Christian, Muslim, Jewish — ideology-targeted, not religion-targeted). The Pulpit faction includes the Settlement Ideologue and the Jihadist Franchise Operator side by side, both funded by Vault charity chains. The satire targets ideology and behavior, not faith.

**Opening sequence ("A Perfectly Normal Morning") — the contextual tutorial.** ~15 minutes. Every system introduced silently through play. The alarm with snoozeable morning slot, the slop fridge with unlabeled BODY ticks, the GnoyNews segment seeding names/logos with no markers, the commute past billboards and a homeless Gnoy with the sign "THEY PUT SOMETHING IN THE FOOD," the job at Greystone Data Solutions (does not exist, processes nothing, 340 employees), the auto-deducted subscriptions, the staged "spontaneous community celebration" at the new Wellness Center, the doctored Feed segment that evening showing the central premise of the game without anyone narrating it, the underground forum bookmark already on the laptop. The day ends, the world ticks, the politburo simulation advances. The player has no idea.

**The Schrödinger's NPC (Dave).** First dialogue choice with the Greystone coworker permanently determines his arc — Default (Gnoy answer) = stays Gnoy forever, heartbreak of resistance / Real answer = future first Recruit, Researcher specialist / Too-awakened = becomes a Glowie even if he wasn't one before. Same first encounter, three radically different futures.

**Voice and writing register:** Sharp, satirical, willing to be funny. Disco Elysium / Fallout DNA, modernized for media-literate audience. No exposition dumps — the world is the information.

### Content Volume

**Locked content scope (subject to development cuts):**

- **7 attributes, 24 skills, 40 talents** (full system locked, talent details TBD in GDD)
- **5 factions, 13 boss archetypes, 1 Inner Ring (size TBD endgame reveal)**
- **12 districts** (5 Tier 1 multi-sub-area, 6 Tier 2 specialty, 1 Tier 3 endgame)
- **5 endings** with distinct triggers and credits sequences

## Art and Audio Direction

### Visual Style

**Satirical Hyper-Realism.** Present-day alt-Earth. Recognizable but slightly wrong.

- **Xyoner / corporate spaces:** colors oversaturated, too vibrant, too clean, too cheerful. McXyon's red is *too* red. NPCs move too uniformly. The artificial cheer is the satire.
- **Slopside / The Hollow:** desaturated, grey-yellow, authentically grimy. *Real* color. Lived-in, melancholic.
- **The contrast IS the art direction.** More Xyoner influence = more artificial vibrancy. The neighborhoods read by palette before they read by text.
- **As Awakening rises:** subtle visual filter shift. What looked cheerful starts reading as *wrong*. NPCs you walked past for 20 hours start looking off.

**Format:** Detailed pixel art OR hand-drawn 2D sprites — not 3D. Final pipeline choice in GDD.

**UI duality:**
- Xyoner-facing UI (feed, map, official city overlays) = polished corporate app aesthetic
- Resistance-facing UI (evidence board, forum, homebase) = hand-made — sticky notes, photos, red string, CRT terminal feel

**References:** *They Live* (normal until you see it), *American Beauty* (perfection over rot), *Don't Look Up* (deadpan satirical present), *Disco Elysium* (color palette, environmental detail).

### Audio Style

**Layered by context:**

- Gnoy daily life: corporate jingles, hold music, upbeat pop slop
- Slopside / Hollow: lo-fi, melancholic, authentic ambient city
- Investigation: slow tension, late-night paranoia (Burial, Arca DNA)
- Combat: aggressive electronic / synthwave (Perturbator, Carpenter Brut, Hotline Miami DNA)
- Xyoner spaces: slick corporate ambient — expensive and empty
- Awakening moments: something cracks in the music production
- Endgame: orchestral-electronic hybrid

**The Music Deterioration Mechanic (signature).** As Awakening Level rises, corporate music in Xyoner spaces audibly deteriorates. Wrong notes early on, jingles that skip by mid-game, corrupted-file artifacts by Awakening 7. The player *hears* the manipulation become visible. This is a signature mechanic. **Budget and pipeline must support multi-version recordings of every Xyoner-space track.**

**Sound as Gameplay:**
- Enemy footsteps audible before visible — stealth is partly an audio game
- Pirate broadcast signal quality represented by audio clarity (Signal Hijack skill = audio fidelity)
- Homebase ambient sound grows from empty-flat-echo to full-resistance-operation hum across the 4 stages

### Production Approach

- **Pixel/2D art:** likely a single primary artist or art director with contractor support for environments/portraits.
- **Audio:** dedicated composer essential given the deterioration mechanic. Sound designer ideally separate-or-shared.
- **Writing:** likely 1 lead writer + contracted dialogue writers for boss arcs. Disco-Elysium-tier word count is the bottleneck.
- **Engineering:** the Politburo Simulation, Dossier System, and Reputation Web each need owned subsystems. Early prototyping mandatory.
- **Vertical slice first.** Lock combat feel + dialogue feel + investigation feel + day cycle in one full district before scaling content.

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Scope.** This is, by content volume and system count, an enormously ambitious indie project. 12 districts × 13 bosses × 5 endings × 24-skill dialogue branching is a *lot*. **Mitigation:** vertical slice first; design supports content cuts (fewer districts, fewer bosses at launch) without breaking the core loop; early-access path is structurally available.
2. **Tone misread.** The game is satire of ideology and oligarchic behavior, not of any group. Misreading risk is real — by reviewers, by streamers, by social media. **Mitigation:** equal-opportunity satire is structurally enforced by the design (multi-faith Pulpit boss roster, ecumenical Vault and Cage, the Xyoners are a fictional class). Communications strategy addresses this proactively.
3. **Writing volume.** Disco-Elysium-tier word count plus 24-skill branching plus internal voices plus conversation-log weaponization is a writing budget that bankrupts unprepared studios. **Mitigation:** lock dialogue scope per encounter early; reuse skill-voice templates; tiered dialogue depth (boss encounters get full treatment, routine NPCs get pared trees).
4. **Patent exposure (WB Nemesis, US 10,926,179).** **Mitigation:** the Dossier / Politburo / Reputation Web triad is structurally distinct (memory in faction not individuals, hierarchy in independent simulation not player actions, individual NPC memory exists but in non-hierarchical Reputation Web). Legal review before launch. Architecture must enforce these distinctions in code.
5. **The Sleep ending (player retention risk).** A game that lets the player give up may be culturally disorienting and could trigger negative emotional reactions. **Mitigation:** this is a feature, not a bug — the existence of the Sleep ending IS the satire. Reviewers should be briefed on it. Marketing should not hide it.
6. **Audio budget under-scoping.** The Music Deterioration Mechanic alone implies multi-version recordings. **Mitigation:** budget the composer line item correctly from day 1; consider scope-tied tiering (5 deterioration stages, not 10).
7. **Marketing readability.** "It's a satirical investigation-CRPG-action hybrid where the conspiracy is real" is a hard 30-second pitch. **Mitigation:** lean on signature mechanics (Music Deterioration, Truth Paradox, Schrödinger's NPC) for short-form content; let the elevator pitch carry the long form.

### Technical Challenges

1. **Politburo Simulation correctness and tractability.** A simulation that runs whether the player exists or not, that produces emergent events the player can intervene in, and that remains coherent across hundreds of in-game weeks. Coarse-grained weekly tick is the design — engineering must validate this works.
2. **Dossier + Reputation Web interaction.** Conversations with Gnoym must be loggable, retrievable, weaponizable, and editable by the Feed. The conversation log is the highest-risk performance/storage area.
3. **Adaptive faction counter-system.** The Dossier must read player build patterns and spawn counter-tactics (thermal sensors vs. ghost builds, counter-narrative vs. broadcast builds). Needs a clean abstraction layer between dossier classification and tactical response.
4. **Investigation UI (3 layers).** Physical Board (drag-and-connect), Dossier Interface (cross-reference + publication), Thought Cabinet (mental threads with Awakening-level reinterpretation). Three custom UI systems with persistent state.
5. **Skill check / dialogue branching infrastructure.** Visible-DC, hidden-consequence, internal-voice-on-skill-trigger, item-drop-into-dialogue. Needs robust dialogue tooling.
6. **Hotline-Miami-grade combat feel in an RPG context.** Lethality + instant restart + stat modulation without becoming either bullet-sponge-y or unfair.
7. **Music deterioration pipeline.** Multi-version audio assets, smooth crossfading per Awakening level, no jarring transitions.

### Market Risks

1. **Genre niche size.** Investigation-CRPG audience is loyal but not massive. Mitigated by combat layer expanding reach.
2. **Cultural moment shift.** Themes are timely now — they may read differently in 3–5 years. Mitigated by the satire being structural, not topical (no real names, no real events).
3. **Storefront / platform politics.** Satirical political content carries platform-flagging risk (Steam, console certification). **Mitigation:** the satire targets fictional factions, not real entities. Legal review of edge cases.
4. **Streaming / clip-economy fit.** Long-form CRPGs are streaming-friendly but not viral-clip-friendly. **Mitigation:** signature mechanics (Music Deterioration moments, Schrödinger's NPC reveals, Boss dialogue confrontations) are designed to be clippable.
5. **Reviewer alignment.** Politically satirical RPGs polarize critics. **Mitigation:** lean into the polarization as press hook; structural balance in the satire forecloses bad-faith dismissal.

### Mitigation Strategies

- **Scope tiering.** Launch-minimum content (4 districts, 5 bosses, 1 ending playable + Sleep ending) versus full-vision content (12/13/5). Cuts identified in advance, not improvised under pressure.
- **Vertical slice gate.** No content scaling past the first complete district until combat + dialogue + investigation + day cycle all feel right in isolation and in combination.
- **Early access option.** The design supports it — district-by-district content drops are natural milestones.
- **Legal review on the Dossier system pre-launch.** Architecture docs must demonstrate the structural distinctions from US 10,926,179.
- **Tone calibration via early playtests.** External eyes on the satire before cultural-context drift makes it unreadable.
- **Audio pipeline locked at vertical slice.** Music deterioration mechanic budgeted and tooled before scaling.
- **Writing pipeline tooling first.** Dialogue tooling, skill-check tooling, internal-voice tooling, conversation-log tooling — before scaling word count.

## Success Criteria

### MVP Definition

**Vertical Slice MVP:**
- One complete district (recommended: Slopside + Cubicle Belt corridor, including Greystone)
- Full opening sequence ("A Perfectly Normal Morning") shippable
- 7 attributes + ~10 skills active and balanced
- Investigation UI all 3 layers functional at Stage 1 homebase
- Hotline-Miami combat loop with capture mechanic
- Day cycle with finite slots, snooze cost, subscriptions, sleep tick
- 1 boss investigation playable end-to-end (recommended: Fact Checker — pure documentation defeat, low combat dependency)
- Dossier system tracking player actions, Reputation Web tracking 5–10 named Gnoym
- Music Deterioration prototype on at least one Xyoner-space track
- ~3 hours of curated play

**Early Access Launch Target:**
- 3–4 districts
- Full RPG systems (24 skills, 40 talents)
- 4–5 boss investigations playable
- 1 ending fully playable (recommended: The Public Reckoning, since it stress-tests the most systems) + Sleep ending available
- Homebase Stages 1–2 complete, Stage 3 stubbed
- Politburo Simulation running

**Full Launch Target:**
- All 12 districts
- All 13 bosses
- All 5 endings
- All 4 homebase stages
- Full Inner Ring endgame structure

### Success Metrics

- **Critical:** Reviews aligned with the satirical-CRPG niche (Disco Elysium / Pentiment / Citizen Sleeper review tier — 85+ on Metacritic among receptive reviewers; aware that mainstream review aggregates may underweight the satire).
- **Cultural:** The Music Deterioration Mechanic and Schrödinger's NPC system spawn organic streamer / TikTok / forum content. Players *describe the game* unprompted using signature mechanics.
- **Commercial:** Solo / small-indie profitability requires modest unit volumes — break-even in the low-tens-of-thousands of unit sales given the design's cost structure. Stretch goal: hundred-thousand-plus over 18 months.
- **Replayability:** Median player attempts 2+ playthroughs (Schrödinger's NPC + 5 endings + radically different builds make this designable).
- **Wishlist conversion (Steam):** Standard CRPG benchmarks apply (~10–20% wishlist→purchase at launch among comparable titles).

### Launch Goals

1. **Steam Early Access launch with 4-district vertical content,** capturing the audience that wants in early on a unique RPG.
2. **Press preview cycle anchored on the signature mechanics** (Truth Paradox, Music Deterioration, Schrödinger's NPC, Sleep ending).
3. **Streaming partnerships with CRPG-friendly creators** (the audience overlap is significant) and tactical satire-friendly creators.
4. **Deliberate non-courting of partisan-political channels** of any persuasion. The game reads to no political tribe; marketing should be similarly disciplined.
5. **Roadmap public from Early Access launch** so players know the full vision and can choose their entry point.
