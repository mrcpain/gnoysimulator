The Pitch

One paragraph. Copy it.

Gnoy Simulator is a top-down satirical RPG set in an alt-Earth present day where manufactured consensus has replaced genuine reality. You play a person who has begun to notice — and noticing, in this world, is dangerous. The game combines real-time combat, a multi-voice inner dialogue system, a political faction simulation, and a full investigation board into a single layered experience built around one question: what does it cost to stay awake when everything around you is designed to keep you asleep? Five distinct endings, 13 hand-crafted villains, and a reputation web that tracks every choice you make across 12 city districts. Currently in active development for PC.

What Makes It Different

Three systems nobody else is building.

✶
Inner Voices Dialogue

You Argue With Yourself

Every dialogue choice surfaces competing internal voices — Body, Mind, Soul, Mouth, Ghost, Signal — each pulling in different directions. Your character's build determines which voices speak and how loud. Conversations are three-way debates before a single word leaves your mouth. It is a dialogue system that makes the cost of speaking visible.

★
Politburo Simulation

The System Has a Board

Five ruling factions control 12 city districts and hold seats on a Politburo that governs the game world in real time. Their alliances shift, their agendas conflict, and your actions move their standing up or down. You are not fighting a villain. You are navigating a power structure that was running long before you arrived and will keep running if you lose.

●
Awakening Track

Awareness Is a Resource

A 10-level deterioration track measures how awake you are — and punishes you for it. High awakening unlocks deeper truths, harder choices, and better endings. It also makes the world actively hostile. The game does not reward being comfortably numb, but it does make comfort tempting. Every level is a mechanical tradeoff, not just a narrative one.
