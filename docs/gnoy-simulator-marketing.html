// SIGNAL HIJACK — THE THING THE AD IS ACTUALLY ABOUT //

You scrolled this far. Good. The combo meal pitch above is real — those are the actual chunks of the game — but the tone is the joke. Gnoy Simulator is a satirical CRPG about waking up inside a corporate-dystopian alt-Earth where the slop, the Feed, the wage cage, and the ruling class are all the same machinery, and the people who notice get called crazy on the news that night.

It's Disco Elysium dialogue depth, Hotline Miami combat, Persona day cycle, Pokémon top-down camera, and Fallout dark humor — squeezed into one game by one developer, with the bit that the corporate UI deteriorates as you wake up. The McXyon's jingle warbles. Your HUD glitches. The world starts looking wrong.

Three games run on the same skeleton: an investigation game (red string on a corkboard), a life simulator (rent, slop, Cope, Fatigue), and an action game (top-down lethal combat where stats matter but never save you). The world simulates the ruling class whether you exist or not. Individual people remember your exact words. The Truth Paradox: the closer you get to right, the more dangerous and discredited you become.

It's the game where giving up is a real ending, and finishing is its own kind of grief.
