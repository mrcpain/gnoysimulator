Receipts ✓
A satirical top-down RPG

Gnoy
Simulator.

You wake up at 6:47 AM. The alarm is selling you something. You believe it.
For now.

Disco Elysium's brain. Hotline Miami's hands. Persona's clock. Shadow of Mordor's memory. Pokémon's camera. One alt-Earth dimension where the slop, the Feed, and the ruling class are the same machinery — and the people who notice get called crazy on the news that night.

I · THE PREMISE

You start the game as the model citizen. You will not finish it that way.

Your name is up to you. Your job isn't. You clock into Greystone Data Solutions, eat a fridge-cold McXyon's, and scroll the Feed on the commute. Rent and seven slop subscriptions auto-deduct each month. Your stats start with Body 4, Mind 2, Soul 2, Mouth 3, Ghost 1, Gut 5, Signal 0 — the numerical shape of a wage-earning gnoy. You are, by every available metric, doing fine.

Then a news event happens in front of you. You watch it happen. You watch the same event on the Feed that night. They are not the same event. The footage has been doctored. A coworker — Dave, whose opinion you do not yet know matters — asks what you think. Whatever you answer permanently locks his fate. And the game, very quietly, begins.

II · THE WORLD

An alt-Earth present day. Familiar enough to hurt.

Twelve districts you can walk. Slopside, where you live. The Cubicle Belt, where you work. The Plaza, where the cameras are. The Vault District. The Garden, where they live. The Pulpit Quarter. University Row. The Hollow — eventually, your safehouse. The Compound — eventually, the endgame.

Each district has a faction in charge of its weather. Each one has its own palette, its own crowd ambience, its own Heat meter that tracks how loud you've been there lately. Get loud in one and the others stay cool. Rotating between them is how mature players survive.

The Five Factions of the Xyoners

The Trough Food & Land

The ones who feed you and own the dirt your apartment building sits on. McXyon's, SloppFlix-Eats, the agribusiness combines, the developers who priced out the neighborhood three mayors ago.

The Feed Media & Algorithm

Trusted Anchors, Engagement Architects, "Fact" Checkers. They do not lie to you. They simply choose the camera angle, the soundbite, the next thing you see. The narrative is the weapon.

The Pulpit Ideology

The Prosperity Prophet. The Think Tank Sage. The Settlement Ideologue. Equal-opportunity satire — every faith and political faction has a profitable parasite, and the Pulpit collects them all.

The Cage Enforcement & Surveillance

The people building a single shared faction file with your name on it. Operatives, Glowies, the contractor your boss reports to. They have a pre-existing rivalry with The Feed — that's useful, if you can survive long enough to use it.

The Vault Finance

Benevolent Billionaires. Debt Dealers. The Number Go Up Guy. They never become fully hostile, which is the most unsettling thing about them.

Above the factions sits The Inner Ring — an unknown number of members, referred to in leaked documents only as "The Arrangement." You discover their names by accident. One of them has been feeding you intel the whole game to eliminate their rivals. You will not know which one until you decide it's over.

The closer you get to right, the more dangerous and discredited you become. — The Truth Paradox · Design Pillar №1
III · THREE GAMES, ONE CHARACTER

Investigator. Wageworker. Operative. All you. All at once.

The same character sheet runs three different games stacked on top of each other. You choose your hours.

The Investigator

Corkboard. Red string. Stamp.

A diegetic conspiracy wall in your apartment. Pin a photo, drag a string to another photo — a skill check fires. Critical success gets you a solid line and a piece of hidden context. Failure gets a dotted line you can still publish, at a cost. Then the Dossier: choose platform × framing × audience and watch the Credibility-vs-Heat meters dance before you commit. No undo. Publication is permanent.

The Wageworker

Day slots. Rent. Cope.

Morning, Afternoon, Evening, optional risky Night. Snooze and the Morning is silently gone. Sleep ticks the world forward one day, deducts your slop subscriptions, fires the Politburo simulation, partially restores Cope. Cancel a subscription and you'll get an "anti-subscription extremism" Feed segment about you inside the hour. Quitting your job is a major milestone.

The Operative

Top-down. Lethal. Instant restart.

Real-time combat in the Hotline Miami school. Stats modify the dials — Body, Hands, Gymmaxx — but never grant invincibility. When you go down, you choose: YIELD (managed capture) or ATTEMPT ESCAPE. Get captured and you wake up in a Cage holding cell off-map. The world remembers every restart: broken doors stay broken, corpses stay where they fell.

IV · THE AWAKENING

It is not a level-up screen. It is a slow tearing.

You have a hidden secondary progression called the Awakening Track, from 1 to 10. It is never shown as a number. You will feel it.

As your Awakening rises, the world literally desaturates around you. A post-process filter peels the brightness off Xyoner spaces — the cheerful starts reading as wrong. At the same time, the corporate music in those spaces audibly deteriorates across five tiers. The McXyon's jingle, on day one, is perfect. By Awakening 5 it is wobbly and slightly off-key. By 7 it is corrupted, skipping, glitching. By 10 it has collapsed entirely into the endgame soundscape.

Your HUD glitches with it. Resistance UI elements — sticky notes, red marker, hand-typed labels — begin replacing the corporate sans and clean cards. The UI itself is awakening, and so are you.

[ Glowie Sense ] He's wearing the wrong shoes for a Trough mid-level. Look at the laces.
[ Rabbit Hole ] Greystone's parent company filed a Q3 disclosure two weeks before the Incident. Two. Weeks.
[ NPC Mode ] Just smile. Just smile. don't break eye contact like that just smile.
[ Cope ] If you say it out loud the room will get smaller. The room is already small.
[ Yap Game ] Push back. He's three sentences from contradicting himself. DC 14.

Twenty-four named Inner Voices live in your head — each with its own font, color, and (when funded) voice cue. Glowie Sense, Rabbit Hole, Lore Depth, Yap Game, NPC Mode, Cope, Ratio, Clout. At low Awakening they are quiet. At high Awakening + high Fatigue they become a full chorus, and you become an unbelievably sharp investigator who can no longer pass for normal. Sharp or sociable. Pick one.

V · THE WORLD WATCHES BACK

Three systems remember you. None of them are kind.

Player Dossier

One faction file. Your whole life.

The Cage compiles a single shared faction file from operative reports, leaks, your own publications, and Gnoym interrogations. It tracks your Reach, your Awareness, the embarrassment you've caused, and a build classifier that tags you as Ghost / Broadcast / Hands / Grifter / Mixed. The OPSEC skill buys you delay; it never buys you invisibility.

Politburo Simulation

The ruling class runs on its own clock.

A weekly deterministic simulation of thirteen boss operatives, five factions, and the Inner Ring — running whether you exist or not. Bosses succeed each other. Blackmail debts come due. Alliances form and break. Your actions feed into this sim as inputs; the simulation's rules decide what happens next.

Reputation Web

People remember your exact words.

Every individual Gnoym you talk to remembers what you said — verbatim. They gossip with their neighbors. A rumor planted in The Hollow takes in-game weeks to reach The Cubicle Belt. When the Cage interrogates one of them, their personal memory of you flows directly into your dossier. Silence registers as evasion.

Schrödinger's NPCs

Dave is alive in three states until you speak.

Some characters' fate is locked by your first conversation with them. Dave, your Greystone coworker, is the loud example. Answer like a gnoy and he stays a gnoy forever — the quiet heartbreak of the resistance. Answer honestly and he becomes your first recruit. Answer too sharply and you learn what Greystone really is.

VI · WHO YOU BUILD

Seven attributes. Twenty-four skills. Forty talents.

BODY · MIND · SOUL · MOUTH · GHOST · GUT · SIGNAL. Build a Ghost-Mode infiltrator and the Cage deploys thermal sensors. Build a Broadcast-heavy Reach monster and the Feed builds a counter-narrative twice as fast. The faction reads you, then responds. The game does not have a "correct" build. It has builds the world has prepared a response for.

Twenty-four named skills, four per attribute — Rabbit Hole, Doxcraft, Glowie Sense, Yap Game, Rizz, Ratio, Ghost Mode, Normie Cosplay, Receipts, OPSEC, Gymmaxx, Hands, Anti-Slop, Web, Ghost Protocol, Signal Hijack. Forty talents across seven archetypes. Some are mutually exclusive — Combat / Stealth / Information Warfare / Network / Awakening / Economy / Wildcard.

Combat itself is honest. Lethal. Restart in under half a second when you die. Six signature satirical weapons blur the line between item and gag: the Evidence Brick stuns a normie for four seconds, the Slop Bag distracts them for six while the Cage ignores it entirely, the Megaphone can cause low-level enforcers to defect, the Signal Jammer cuts Cage backup for thirty seconds, the Camera Flash blinds and generates Receipts-grade evidence, the Fake ID Packet makes the NPCs verify each other for ten seconds. Satire as tactics.

VII · THE CAST OF VILLAINS

Thirteen operatives. Each one only dies once.

Some you beat with documents. Some with dialogue, in a multi-stage composure-HP duel where wrong framings cost you evidence and Heat. Some by handing them to a rival faction and letting them eat each other. Defeat is permanent. The Politburo will fill the role with a successor — but that individual is gone forever.

VIII · HOW IT ENDS

Five endings. You decide when. The game does not push you.

There is no boss-rush gate. There is no point-of-no-return cinematic. When your Inner Ring Confidence is high enough and your disposition axes are positioned, you commit. The game does not tell you you're ready. The game does not tell you you're not.

The Public Reckoning

You go public. The dam breaks. The Feed loses the narrative for forty-eight hours. Requires high Awake, high Rebel, high Reach.

The Shadow Replacement

You don't expose the Arrangement. You replace it. Same machinery, new hand on the lever. Requires high Rebel, low public footprint.

The Burn

You destroy what you can reach. You take no successor's seat. You will not be remembered correctly. Requires high Awake + Rebel, low Reach.

The Long Game

You walk away. You build something quiet. You touch grass. Requires high Soul, high Web, the Touch-Grass trait. The most patient ending.

The Sleep

You give up. You re-subscribe to SloppFlix. You eat the slop. The credits roll over your apartment, exactly as you found it.
This is a real ending. The game does not punish you. The game does not nudge you away from it. It is structurally supported and seriously written, because some people, in the actual world, choose it.

IX · WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE

Satirical hyper-realism. Two visual languages. One satire.

Xyoner spaces — the corporate office, the McXyon's, the Plaza — are oversaturated, too vibrant, too clean. Geometric sans. Polished cards. The aesthetic of a Series A pitch deck.

Resistance spaces — your apartment, the Hollow, the underground forum — are aged paper, hand-typed, marker-and-pencil annotated, sticky-noted, slightly crooked. The aesthetic of a private investigator's bathroom.

The duality itself is the satire. Both share one component grammar underneath. Both decay as you Awaken. The HUD does not narrate — the world does.
